## How to Evaluate Kratom Vendors Before You Buy

Not every vendor on a "best of" list will be the right fit for every buyer. Here's a practical framework for making your own assessment.

**Check for lab testing first.** Any vendor worth considering should test every batch through an independent, third-party lab. The test results — called Certificates of Analysis (COAs) — should show mitragynine content (typically 1.0–1.8% for quality leaf), 7-hydroxymitragynine levels, and screening for contaminants including heavy metals, E. coli, salmonella, and pesticides. If a vendor can't produce current COAs, try this instead: move on to one that can.

**Verify AKA GMP status.** The American Kratom Association's GMP Standards Program is the closest thing the industry has to a quality benchmark [8]. It's not perfect, and it's voluntary, but vendors who maintain certification are submitting to audits and following standardized manufacturing protocols. That matters.

**Read the return policy.** Reputable vendors stand behind their products. A clear, reasonable return policy signals confidence in product quality. Vendors with no-return policies or excessive restocking fees are best avoided.

**Compare pricing with context.** Raw price comparisons don't tell the full story. A $60 kilo with no lab testing is not a better deal than a $90 kilo with full COAs and AKA certification. Premium kratom products command higher pricing for real reasons — testing, compliance, and sourcing all cost money [2].

## Common Mistakes When Choosing a Kratom Vendor

**Chasing the lowest price.** Commodity kratom is cheap for a reason. Skipping lab testing, using lower-grade leaf, and cutting corners on storage and handling all reduce costs — and quality. If a price seems too good to be true, it usually doesn't hold up under scrutiny.

**Ignoring batch variation.** Even good vendors can have off batches. The best vendors test every batch individually and publish results. Vendors who test "periodically" or "representative samples" leave gaps where inconsistent product can slip through.

**Buying from unverified marketplaces.** Third-party marketplace sellers on major e-commerce platforms often lack any quality verification. Direct-to-consumer purchases from established vendor websites provide more accountability.

**Not rotating strains.** This isn't a vendor issue, but it affects vendor satisfaction. Using the same strain daily accelerates tolerance. Rotating between 2–3 strains helps maintain effectiveness and reduces the likelihood of blaming a vendor for what's actually a tolerance problem.

**Skipping the research.** Five minutes reviewing a vendor's lab testing page, AKA certification status, and return policy can save weeks of frustration with subpar product.

## Safety Considerations Every Buyer Should Know

Kratom is a botanical product with real physiological effects. Responsible use matters.

- **Talk to a doctor** before using kratom, especially if you take prescription medications. Kratom's alkaloids interact with certain drug classes, including opioids, benzodiazepines, and some antidepressants.
- **Start low.** 2 grams is a reasonable first dose for powder. Wait 45–60 minutes before considering a second dose. Effects vary significantly by individual body weight, metabolism, and tolerance.
- **Stay hydrated.** Kratom can be dehydrating. Increase water intake on days you use it.
- **Check local laws.** Kratom legality varies by jurisdiction and continues to change [5][8]. Verify your state and local regulations before purchasing.
- **Don't mix with alcohol or other substances.** Combining kratom with depressants increases risk of adverse effects.
- **Buy tested product.** Untested kratom may contain heavy metals, bacteria, or adulterants. This is the single most important safety step, and it starts with choosing a reputable vendor.
